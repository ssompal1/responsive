Redesigning the NYC DOB Search Site

Improving simplicity, usability, and accessibility for community use

Background

Last year, I learned from a grassroots collective of Asian migrant workers that they used the NYC DOB Search site to check if their workplaces had city violations. While somewhat efficient, the site had severe usability and accessibility shortcomings.

Usability Issues

  • Efficient but hard to learn: All inputs were on one page, but lack of guidance made first-time use difficult.
  • Poor hierarchy: Small titles and same-sized text confused navigation.
  • Low memorability: Few visual cues helped repeat users remember steps.

Accessibility Audit

I used the WebAIM WAVE tool to evaluate the site. Major findings included lack of alt text, missing form labels, and poor screenreader navigation due to layout tables.

User Impact

  • Screenreader users couldn't complete searches.
  • New users were intimidated by unclear design.
  • Massage workers reported difficulty accessing needed information.
